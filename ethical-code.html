Professional Standards

Ethical Code

Interlove is built on confidentiality, dignity, client autonomy, and honest expectations. This code explains the principles that guide coaching and client care.

Confidentiality Boundaries Autonomy Inclusivity Referrals Integrity

Confidentiality and Privacy

Clients deserve a private space to explore personal questions honestly. Personal information, session details, and sensitive stories are treated as confidential and are not shared publicly without explicit permission.

Client examples, testimonials, or educational material are only used when consent has been given or when identifying details have been removed.

Professional Boundaries

Coaching is a professional relationship. Interlove does not pursue romantic, sexual, financial, or exploitative relationships with clients.

The role of coaching is to support reflection, growth, communication, and aligned action. It is not therapy, medical treatment, legal advice, or a substitute for licensed clinical care.

Client Autonomy

Clients remain responsible for their choices. Coaching may offer questions, frameworks, reflection, and guidance, but it should never override your values, consent, intuition, or personal agency.

You are free to question, pause, reject, or adapt any suggestion that does not feel right for your life.

Inclusivity and Respect

Interlove welcomes clients across cultures, identities, orientations, life stages, and relationship structures. Coaching is not built around shame, stereotypes, or one narrow idea of what love should look like.

Feedback is offered with care. The goal is clarity and growth, never humiliation, manipulation, or pressure.

Appropriate Referrals

If a client would be better supported by a therapist, physician, legal professional, crisis service, or another specialist, Interlove will encourage that support rather than stretching coaching beyond its appropriate scope.

Coaching can work alongside therapy when appropriate, but it does not replace it.

Honest Marketing and Expectations

Interlove avoids fear-based messaging, guaranteed romantic outcomes, and pressure tactics. Coaching can support meaningful change, but no ethical coach can promise a partner, marriage, reconciliation, or another person's behavior.

Testimonials and client reflections are presented responsibly and with consent. They are examples of individual experience, not promises of identical results.

Accountability

Feedback and concerns are welcome. If something in the coaching relationship feels unclear or uncomfortable, clients are encouraged to raise it directly so it can be addressed with care.

Questions about this code can be sent to info@interlove.co.
